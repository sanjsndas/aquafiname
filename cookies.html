1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, as well as to provide reporting information and to remember your preferences. Cookies are essentially a website's "memory," allowing it to recognize your device on subsequent visits and recall information about your previous interactions, such as items added to a shopping cart, login details, or language preferences. This recognition helps to streamline your browsing experience, making it faster and more convenient.

There are different types of cookies, each serving distinct functions. Some cookies are essential for a website to operate correctly, enabling core functionalities like secure logins or navigation. Others are used to analyze website performance, track user behavior, or personalize content and advertisements. Cookies can be set by the website you are visiting (first-party cookies) or by third-party services that the website uses (third-party cookies). The duration for which a cookie remains on your device can also vary; some expire after a single browsing session (session cookies), while others persist for a longer, predefined period (persistent cookies).

The information stored within cookies is typically anonymous and does not directly identify you as an individual. However, in some cases, cookie data can be linked with other information to create a profile of your browsing habits. This is why understanding and managing your cookie preferences is crucial for maintaining your online privacy. Modern web browsers offer robust tools for users to control cookie settings, allowing you to accept, reject, or delete cookies, either globally or on a site-by-site basis. Aquafiname is committed to respecting your choices regarding cookies and providing you with the necessary tools and information to manage them effectively.

3. How Long Do Cookies Last?

The duration for which a cookie remains on your computer or mobile device depends on whether it is a "session" cookie or a "persistent" cookie:

  • Session Cookies: These are temporary cookies that remain on your device only for as long as you keep your browser open. They are typically used for essential functions, such as enabling secure login sessions or remembering items in a shopping cart during a single visit. Once you close your browser, session cookies are automatically deleted. For Aquafiname, session cookies might be used to maintain your login state on our smart monitoring dashboard, ensuring you don't have to re-enter your credentials repeatedly during a single browsing session. They are crucial for the seamless navigation and functionality of our website during your active visit, providing a fluid and uninterrupted experience. The ephemeral nature of session cookies means they do not store long-term information about your browsing habits, focusing solely on facilitating your current interaction with the website. This type of cookie is fundamental for ensuring that our website operates efficiently and securely for the duration of your visit, contributing to a smooth and reliable user experience.
  • Persistent Cookies: These cookies remain on your device for a longer period, even after you close your browser. They have a specific expiration date, which can range from a few minutes to several years, depending on their purpose. Persistent cookies are used to remember your preferences, login details, or to track your browsing behavior over time. For Aquafiname, persistent cookies might be used to remember your language preference for future visits, or to recall your cookie consent choices so you don't see the consent banner every time you return. They are also commonly used by analytics and advertising services to track user interactions over multiple visits, helping us understand long-term trends and personalize content. The extended lifespan of persistent cookies allows for a more personalized and convenient experience across multiple interactions with our website. For example, if you frequently visit our website to check updates on rainwater harvesting technology, a persistent cookie might remember your preferences, ensuring that relevant content is displayed more prominently. These cookies are vital for enhancing user experience over time and for supporting our marketing and analytical efforts. You have the ability to delete persistent cookies manually through your browser settings at any time before their expiration date. Understanding the distinction between session and persistent cookies empowers you to better manage your online privacy and browsing preferences, giving you control over how long your data is stored and used for various purposes by Aquafiname and its partners.

  • Block third-party cookies: This will prevent cookies from being set by domains other than the one you are currently visiting. This can help reduce tracking by advertising networks.
  • Delete existing cookies: You can clear all cookies stored on your device at any time.
  • Receive a notification before a cookie is set: Your browser will prompt you before accepting a new cookie, allowing you to decide whether to allow it.
  • Manage cookies on a site-by-site basis: Some browsers allow you to specify cookie preferences for individual websites.

The method for managing cookies varies depending on the browser you use. For detailed instructions, please refer to your browser's official help pages:

  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Microsoft Edge
  • Apple Safari
  • Opera

Please note that if you delete cookies or modify your browser settings, you may need to re-enter certain information or adjust preferences on subsequent visits to our website. 7. Other Tracking Technologies (Web Beacons, Pixels)

In addition to cookies, Aquafiname may use other similar tracking technologies, such as web beacons (also known as "pixel tags" or "clear GIFs") and pixels. These are tiny graphics files that contain a unique identifier that enable us to recognize when someone has visited our website or opened an email that we have sent them. This allows us, for example, to monitor the traffic patterns of users from one page within our website to another, to deliver or communicate with cookies, to understand whether you have come to our website from an online advertisement displayed on a third-party website, and to improve site performance. Web beacons are often used in conjunction with cookies; if you disable cookies, web beacons may still be able to collect anonymous information about your visits, but they will not be able to link that information to your specific user profile.

We use these technologies to gain a more comprehensive understanding of how our website and marketing communications are performing. For instance, web beacons in emails help us track whether our newsletters are opened and if links within them are clicked, allowing us to refine our communication strategies and deliver more engaging content. Pixels placed on our website can help us understand which advertisements are most effective in driving traffic and conversions, enabling us to optimize our advertising spend. While these technologies primarily collect anonymous, aggregated data, when combined with other information, they can provide insights into individual user behavior. We are committed to using these tracking technologies responsibly and in compliance with all applicable data privacy laws. Your privacy is important to us, and we strive to ensure that our use of these tools is transparent and aligned with your expectations. If you have concerns about these technologies, managing your browser's cookie settings (as described in Section 5.1) will often limit their functionality, as they frequently rely on cookies to operate effectively.
